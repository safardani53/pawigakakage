Which horse does Rachael Blackmore ride in the Grand National 2023?

RACHAEL BLACKMORE is all the rage with punters ahead of the Grand National.

The 2021 winner - who made history with her victory on Minella Times - is being backed off the boards to win the world's most famous race for a second time.

What horse is Rachael Blackmore riding in the Grand National?

Blackmore will ride Ain't That A Shame in the Grand National.

The nine-year-old gelding is trained by Henry de Bromhead and owned by Robcour.

The horse was the subject of a surge of market support on Friday.

Ain't That A Shame has been backed from 14-1 into 8-1 favouritism.

Gordon Elliott's Delta Work - who won the Cross Country at Cheltenham Festival - can be found at a similar price.

Ain't That A Shame has won connections just over £40,000 from ten races.

Ain't That A Shame has a very interesting profile for a National runner.

He is relatively lightly raced with just seven chase runs.

And he has never gone beyond three miles - the National will be run over 4m2½f.

The race will be off at 5.15pm.

What the bookies say

On the 50th anniversary of the legendary Red Rum’s first victory in the race, the enduring popularity of the Grand National will once again see millions of people across the UK place their annual flutter on the Aintree highlight this weekend, with bookmaker Coral estimating there will be up to £150 million bet by the time the 40 runners line up at the start at 5.15pm on Saturday.

Coral’s David Stevens said: "There is simply no other day like Grand National day in the British sporting calendar, not only does it attract by far the biggest TV audience of the year for a horserace, but millions of people will enjoy having their traditional once a year flutter, leading to total turnover on the race of £150 million.

"Blackmore made history when becoming the first female jockey to win the race two years ago and could have another big chance on Ain’t That A Shame, with her mount already backed into favouritism.

"Football manager and I’m A Celeb winner Harry Redknapp co-owns Back On The Lash, whose odds have already halved from 50-1 to 25-1, while on the 50th anniversary of Red Rum’s first victory, Ginger McCain’s son Donald will be looking to add to the family’s record in the race when he saddles Minella Trump,” concluded Stevens."
