Theresa May has found a novel way of cutting costs after shoe-loving PM given discount to high-e

THERESA May has found a novel way of cutting costs – after the shoe-loving Prime Minister was given a discount card for the high-end retailer Russell & Bromley.

She is famous for her love of designer footwear - and wore shoes from the British firm to both of her speeches at the Conservative Party conference.

The detail was listed in register of MPs’ interests – and is valid from January 2017 to January 2018, according to the official document.

She was previously given the discount card by Russell and Bromley in 2014 and 2015, as well as one to the designer fashion firm Amanda Wakeley.

The latest version also reveals Ed Miliband was paid a lot less than Jeremy Vine when he stood in for him in Radio 2.

The former Labour leader said he was paid £2,500 for hosting the afternoon show for a week last month, while the regular presenter makes £700,000 a year from the BBC.

Iain Duncan Smith, the ex-Tory leader, who also hosted the programme for a week, is yet to declare his payment – leaving it unclear as to whether they were given the same salary.

One MP who is earning a lot of money outside of Parliament is Nadine Dorries – who registered that she will be make £13,000 a month for writing her books.

The Conservative member for Mid Bedfordshire will also receive a £50,000 advance for a new book deal.

Other notable entries on the latest register of interests, covering those registered from July 10 to July 31, include two tickets for Commons Speaker John Bercow in the Royal Box at Wimbledon - worth an estimated £8,590.

MOST READ IN POLITICS:

Another bizarre entry in the register came from Tory MP Sir Greg Knight - who was paid £620 to perform alongside other politicians in a rock band called MP4 which appeared on Dave TV's Unspun last year.

And it was revealed the Labour MP for Tooting, Dr Rosena  Allin-Khan, was paid for two shifts working at her local hospital, and Damian Collins – the chair of the Culture, Media and Sport Select Committee, was one of several MPs to declare hospitality to the Glastonbury music festival.
