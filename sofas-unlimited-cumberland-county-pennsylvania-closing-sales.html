Major retailer going out of business forever after three decades with huge deals between 50-65% off

A MAJOR retailer is going out of business and offering huge sales as its owners seek to get rid of all of their inventory.

Sofas Unlimited in Cumberland County, Pennsylvania is closing its doors for good after 31 years in business.

Owner Jack Lashay took to Facebook on March 30 to inform shoppers that the retailer is closing for good.

"Sofas Unlimited is GOING OUT OF BUSINESS FOREVER! Yes, after 31 years we’re closing our doors and LIQUIDATING OUR ENTIRE INVENTORY!" the owner wrote in the post.

"Everything for your home SOLD AT A FRACTION OF THE COST! Rush to 4713 Carlisle Pike, Mechanicsburg – Between Red Lobster and Arooga’s Grille!"

Some items up for sale have been listed on the retailer's Facebook page including recliners, dining furniture, living room furniture, home décor, and more.

Sales include 65 percent off area rugs, 50 percent off accessories, and “sacrifice prices” on every other piece of furniture.

The business applied for its closure on March 29 and now has 30 days to close completely or apply for a 30-day extension, according to store manager Martin Goffin.

Goffin offered several reasons as to why the store is closing, noting that owner Lashay recently celebrated his 80th birthday.

He said the building is also being sold to new owners and there was no successor in the family to take over the business after Lashay.

At this time it is currently not known who will take over after the family-owned business closes down.

Customers can visit Sofas Unlimited to cash in on the huge deals from Monday through Saturday from 10am to 8pm and Sundays from 12pm to 5pm.
