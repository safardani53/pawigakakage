How to Customize and Color Your Own Mug

Tailored mugs and cups look great but feel greater if you get to personalize them all by yourself. Here’s how you can create your own photo mug:

Pick the mug

Before you start, you need to pick the appropriate mug. You can go for standard 11oz, 15oz medium mugs, or 20oz large ones. You can also color your magic mugs and cups. You can pick a coffee mug, a travel mug, a frosted mug, espresso cups for it.

Choose a print or design

The next step would be picking the design or pattern you would want to be printed on your mug. It could be an abstract piece of art, a picture, design pattern, or anything you’d like to put up on the mug.

If you’re designing your own mug online, you can load your own photos from the device. These online customizations also allow you to put multiple pictures on the mug. You can also insert text and small, additional design elements.

Edit

When you’re using an online platform for coloring, you can edit the images, rotate, reposition, zoom, add special effects or multiple filters to the print on your cup. Edit the layout, position, and reposition the image or text the way you would like.

Make it stay

If the mug is hand-painted, cover it with a layer of acrylic finish to seal the colors in or bake for 20 minutes. The reusable and recolorable mugs will wash down colors with water and wipes.

Tips for Hand Painting and Coloring Mugs

When you’re painting a mug, make sure:

  • You wash the mug properly and wipe it with a methylated spirit.
  • Only paint the outside and leave space around the rim.
  • It’s best to draw your design outline with the tip of the brush pen and then hold the pen flatter to fill in.
  • If you’re using one color above the other, let the first one dry completely so the colors don’t mix.
  • Use cotton buds or wipes to correct mistakes.
  • Once done, dry the mug for 15 minutes and put it in a preheated oven at 160-degree-Celcius for 25 minutes.
  • Leave it to cool and you have your mug ready.
