Four cereals you'll never see again - and fans are still mourning the greatest cereal ever

EVEN decades after popular cereals have been discontinued, sweet eaters across the nation are still missing their favorites.

Like many 1990s babies, the sugary stuff was a treat best enjoyed on a Saturday morning watching a favorite cartoon or as a late-night snack.

But the choices today look quite different as many childhood favorites are long gone.

As shoppers scan the supermarket, good luck trying to find Mr. T Cereal, Hidden Treasures, Oreo's O's, and more.

Below, The U.S. Sun has compiled a list of four cereals fans are still drooling over.

1. CHICKEN & WAFFLES

Chicken & Waffles from Post was one of​​ several new cereal flavors the company put out in 2019.

A spin on Honey Bunches of Oats, the cereal had its iconic flakes and clusters but with maple-flavored, waffle-shaped, and drumstick-shaped pieces flavored with real poultry seasoning.

The back of the box described it as: “Sweet & savory unite in a flavor-intense cereal that combines chicken & waffles flavored goodness for a unique breakfast experience!”

While the cereal was only created for National Cereal Day, it was short-lived much to fans’ dismay.

2. CINNAMON MINI BUNS

Kellogg's Cinnamon Mini Buns was a popular cereal back in the 1990s that many companies have tried to recreate since then.

Most read in Money

The cereal was made with corn, whole-grain oats, and a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Television ads described the cereal: "They're like cinnamon buns... the size of cereal.”

Since being discontinued, the company has made many variations of the bite-sized buns, including Mini Swirlz Cereal Cinnamon Bun, Frosted Mini Wheats Cinnamon Roll flavors, and even partnering with Cinnabun.

However, some fans say those don’t compare to the '90s classic.

One Twitter user wrote: “Kellogg's Cinnamon Mini Buns RIP was the greatest cereal ever but it's been discontinued for years!”

3. CHOCO DONUTS

Cap'n Crunch Choco Donuts from Quaker was advertised as the greatest mistake using “Oops!” as its marketing ploy.

The cereal was chocolate rings, in the shape of donuts of course, with colorful sprinkles on top.

While the sugary cereal was advertised as a “mistake,” fans bought it up in droves, which is why it hurt so many when the company discontinued it in the early 2000s.

That didn’t stop the public from getting it back though - or at least trying to.

Dedicated cereal lovers started a petition about a decade ago to bring the chocolatey donuts back.

4. YUMMY MUMMY

General Mills loves a good Monster cereal as in the past it created Franken Berry, Boo Berry, Count Chocula, and more.

Decades ago, the cereal maker tried its hand at something different - mummies.

Originally called Frute Brute, the company later changed it to Fruity Yummy Mummy.

The cereal was around in the late '80s and early '90s and was typically produced as a spooky special from September to October.

Each box was a fruit flavor, frosted cereal with bat marshmallows inside - and sometimes a mystery prize.
