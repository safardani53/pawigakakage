Peoples Vote London march: When is the Final Say rally and what's the route and what time does it

THOUSANDS of protesters are pounding the pavements for the third 'People's Vote March' in London today.

The Final Say rally takes place on Saturday, October 19 - here's all you need to know.

Follow our live blog for latest updates from Saturday's emergency Commons sitting to vote on Boris Johnson's Brexit deal

When and where is the Final Say rally?

More than 170 coaches have been hired to transport campaigners from across the country to the capital.

The crowd is estimated to be 312,000 to 400,000 strong on Saturday.

What's the route?

It starts at noon in Park Lane, marching to Parliament Square where speeches will be held.

MPs, celebrities and influential speakers will deliver their thoughts on the way Brexit has been handled, demanding a second vote.

What's it for?

The 'People's Vote' is being pushed for the government to allow a second referendum so British people can decide if they still wanted to leave the EU.

Boris Johnson has said he is "very confident" MPs will back his last-minute Brexit deal despite the Democratic Unionist Party ruling out its support.

Mr Johnson faces an uphill battle to get the deal backed during an extraordinary sitting of Parliament on Saturday, after his key and influential allies in the DUP rejected it.

The People's Vote website says: "It's now clear that this is a crisis that cannot be settled by our prime minister or his government. It can only be settled by us, the people.
