Who is Val McDermid, what are her books in order, who's her wife Jo Sharp and when did the Tony Hill

IF YOU'RE a fan of crime novels, the chances are you've heard of this woman.

Val McDermid is one of the most popular crime writers in the UK. Here's all you need to know about her and her work...

Who is Val McDermid?

Val McDermid was born on June 4, 1955, in Fife Scotland.

She went to Kirkcaldy High School before going to Oxford University.

After her degree, Val became a journalist and wrote her first novel Report for Murder: The First Lindsay Gordon Mystery in 1987.

She has since become one of the most popular crime writers in the UK and even co-founded the Harrogate Crime Writing Festival.

Who's her wife Jo Sharp?

Val McDermid started seeing Jo Sharp in 2014.

Jo is a professor of geography at Glasgow University.

After two years together the couple tied the knot on October 23, 2016.

MOST READ IN TV & SHOWBIZ

When did the Tony Hill character first appear?

Val's famous character Tony Hill first appeared in 1995 in her novel The Mermaids Singing.

The book was later adapted into the pilot episode of ITV's television series Wire In The Blood starring Robson Green and Hermione Norris.

The book is set in an English city where men are being abducted and tortured using horrific medieval techniques.

The police reluctantly recruited criminal profiler Tony Hill, a mysterious man with secrets to hide.

The killer is revealed in dramatic fashion after Tony himself is kidnapped and almost becomes the latest victim.
