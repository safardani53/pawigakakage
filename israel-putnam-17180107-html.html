He also served as a notable officer as an officer of the Rangers of Rogers during the French and Indian wars, when he was captured by Mohawk warriors. He was saved from an enemy burning ritual due to the intervention of a French officer whose Mohawk was their ally. Putnam's courage and fighting spirit was known far beyond the Connecticut border thanks to the passing of folk legends in the American colonies and states to commemorate his deeds.
