Boozy louts force EasyJet flight from Luton to Tenerife to divert to Portugal after 'throwing drinks

DRUNKEN British louts on an easyJet flight hurled drinks at families and threatened passengers, forcing the plane to be diverted.

Two foul-mouthed thugs swore at parents and kids as they terrified passengers midair on the flight from Luton to Tenerife last Saturday afternoon.

A dad who confronted the yobs - believed to be in their 30s - when they abused his two young children was moved to the front of the plane for his own safety, one flier said.

The jet was diverted to Faro, Portugal so the men could be arrested.

Cops boarded the easyJet plane and dragged off the two yobs, who are said to have punched Portuguese officers on the tarmac as horrified passengers watched on.

The men are said to have downed a bottle of vodka before boarding the flight.

MIDAIR MAYHEM

Roberta Carlton, 73, and husband Michael Carlton, 75, were flying to Tenerife for a 12-day holiday when the troublemakers sparked chaos at around 35,000 feet.

Roberta said: “The police took the two guys off the back of the plane. Someone said they had to drag them off.

“They had a fight with the people on the tarmac, threw a few punches at the policeman, and they took them way. They put them on a bus.

“When the police got on everyone cheered and when they took them off everyone cheered and clapped.

It was awful. There were so many people with babies, it was awful for them. I think they should be banned from flying ever again

Roberta Carlton

“It was awful. There were so many people with babies, it was awful for them. If you are sitting close to them it is threatening.

“I think they should be banned from flying ever again.”

Incredibly, it is the second time in five years the couple, from Barnet, North, London have been on a flight from Luton to Tenerife that diverted to Faro because of drunken louts.

In last Saturday's shocking incident, stewards were forced to intervene when the two drunken men began abusing passengers.

The captain announced over the tannoy that the flight was being diverted to Faro.

'IT IS DANGEROUS'

Retired physiotherapist Roberta added: “I think it is shocking. I think it is dangerous. I think it is inconsiderate.

“They should ban the sale of alcohol in airports.”

She said easyJet air crew were "very, very good" as they dealt with the drunks.

After cops bundled the yobs off the flight at Faro, the plane eventually headed off to the Canary Islands three hours later.

An EasyJet spokesman said: “We can confirm that the Captain of flight EZY2037 from London Luton to Tenerife on 24th August took the decision to divert to Faro due to a group of passengers behaving disruptively.

“The aircraft was met by police on arrival in Faro and the passengers were arrested before the flight continued to Tenerife.
