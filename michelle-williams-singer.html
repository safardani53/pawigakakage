Michelle Williams (Singer) Hollywood Life

Bio:
Michelle Williams (born as Tenitra Michelle Williams on July 24, 1980) is an American singer, songwriter, record producer and actress. As a kid, Michelle never imagined she’d be a part of one of the most successful girl groups of all time. At 17, she became a backup singer for Monica. Three years later, she was told she’d be a part of the group, Destiny’s Child, along with Beyoncé and Kelly Rowland. From then on, she, with the group, won numerous awards, including two Grammys, MTV VMAs, and “Soul Train Lady of Soul” Award. Her first recorded album with the group was “Survivor” in 2001.

Best Known For:
Michelle rose to fame in the 2000s as a member of R&B girl group Destiny’s Child, one of the best-selling female groups of all time.

Personal Life:
Michelle keeps her personal life very private. However, she once described her dating life as “so unfortunate.”
