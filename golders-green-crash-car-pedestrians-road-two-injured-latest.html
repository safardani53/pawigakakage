Golders Green crash - Heartstopping moment car ploughs into pedestrians leaving two injured

THIS is the heartstopping moment a car ploughs into two pedestrians before smashing into a pharmacy.

Two men, in their 40s and 80s, were rushed to hospital after the London crash this morning, but remarkably neither have life-threatening injuries.

The shocking video shows the moment the car careers onto the Golders Green pavement, carrying a man on its bonnet.

An older looking man, walking with a stick, attempts to get out of the way but is swept up in the chaos.

The silver car smashes into the front of the shop, rattling the wall which the camera is mounted on.

Another video caught the amazing efforts of have-a-go heroes attempting to lift the car off one of the injured men in the aftermath.

Moment have-a-go heroes try to lift car off trapped pedestrians after it crashed into Golders Green shop

One person tweeted: "Car driven into shop at Golders Green. People hurt. Pessach shoppers opposite Kosher Kingdom.

"No idea yet if accident or not."

Another posted: "Car crash into a shop on Golders Green Road. Reports of a pedestrian trapped under the car."

A Met Police spokesman said: "Officers, the London Ambulance Service and London Fire Brigade attended and found a car in collision with a pedestrian and a shop.

"Two men, aged in their 40s and 80s, were taken to a central London hospital. Neither have life-threatening injuries.

"Officers remain on scene. No arrests have been made.

"Enquiries continue."

An Air Ambulance was seen landing at Princes Park near to the site of the crash.

A spokeswoman from the London Fire Brigade said: "Two fire engines and a fire rescue unit have been called to a road traffic collision in Golders Green Road.

"One car has collided with a shop front.

"The Brigade was called at 1154. Fire crews from Hendon, Finchley and Paddington fire stations are at the scene."

MPS Barnet tweeted: "Police in #Barnet are on scene and dealing with a road traffic collision at Golders Green Road.

"Road closure is currently in place. The public are thanked for their co-operation."

A London Ambulance spokesman said: “We were called at 11.46am today (28 March) to reports of a road traffic collision on Golders Green Road, NW11.

“We sent a number of resources to the scene including three ambulance crews, an incident response officer and our hazardous area response team. We also dispatched London’s Air Ambulance.

“We treated two people at the scene and both have been taken as a priority to a major trauma centre.”

The A502 Golders Green Road was closed in both directions due to the crash.
