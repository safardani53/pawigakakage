Death of Sam Ehlinger’s Father

Ross Ehlinger, Sam’s father, had traveled to San Francisco to compete in a triathlon. However, he never crossed the finish line.

Sam Ehlinger | Career

With a stellar track record, Sam committed to playing collegiate football for the Texas Longhorns at the University of Texas.

He appeared in nine games as a freshman and threw a school-record 158 passes for 1,915 yards and eleven passing touchdowns (57.5 percent of the team).

The player’s performance gradually improved over time. During his college career, he appeared in 46 games, completing 923 passes (62.5 percent of the team’s total) for 11,436 yards and 94 touchdowns.

During his undergraduate career, Sam was named to Second-Team All-Big 12.

After an outstanding college career, Sam Ehlinger chose to enter the NFL Draft in 2021.

Sam was drafted by Indianapolis Colts as the 218th pick in the sixth round of the 2021 NFL Draft. 

On May 19, 2021, Sam signed a four-year rookie contract with the Colts.

Sam Ehlinger | Charity

In 2020, Ehlinger announced on social media that he had created a GoFundMe page to assist organizations affected by the pandemic both locally and nationally.

“I want to assist people in any way I can,” Ehlinger said in a statement provided to Yahoo Sports. He further added that “It’s an event with so many unknowns, so many ways it’s hurting people, and I feel like we need to help people who are suffering from it.”

The player posted on Twitter and Instagram that the money would be distributed to organizations such as the Boys & Girls Club of America, Austin Pets Alive!, the Central Texas Food Bank, and others. He set a goal of raising $1 million.

Some well-known donors included Texas offensive line coach Herb Hand, offensive coordinator Mike Yurcich, and former offensive coordinator Tim Beck.
