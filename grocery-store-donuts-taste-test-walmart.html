I tested four grocery store donuts, including Walmart - we ranked by texture and it was a close call

A FATHER and son duo took on the difficult task of finding the best donuts from grocery stores, and it wasn't easy.

Nothing is better than walking down the aisles of snacks in a grocery store and picking out your favorite comfort food.

However, not all snacks are made equally as delicious as the others.

Michael and his son Collin took on the challenge of finding the best grocery store donuts.

On their YouTube channel, Collin and Michael taste-test popular foods based on suggestions from viewers.

Michael admitted most people don't think of the grocery store as their go-to donut shop, but when the craving hits it's a satisfying option.

The duo tasted donuts from Walmart, Fred Meyer, WinCo, and Albertsons.

They tried the donuts two at a time to create a final face-off of the most delicious options.

WinCo VS Walmart

The first donuts to square off were from WinCo and Walmart.

Collin said he's pretty familiar with Walmart donuts, but not WinCo.

All the donuts were maple bars, except for the ones from Walmart which came in the regular circle donut shape.

First, they tasted the Walmart donut and agreed that while it was good, it was a little too dense.

They both agreed the donuts were a bit heavy and had a good amount of maple glaze.

The Winco donut immediately got praises. It was lighter and fluffier, had a stronger maple taste, and Michael even said the glaze melted in his mouth.

Since the texture was better on the WinCo donut, it was deemed the winner of this round.

Fred Meyer VS Albertsons

Collin said the Fred Meyer donut looked more homemade and seemed to match WilCo in fluffiness.

As Michael went back for seconds, Collin said it tasted homemade as well and had a different type of maple flavor than the last two they had tried.

The Albertsons donut smelled very sugary and was a little denser, but they both agreed it was delicious.

Albertsons came out on top in this round.

WinCo VS Albertsons

In the big finale, Michael and Collin agreed the maple in WinCo donut had an odd taste and was a bit grainy.

The Albertsons donut held up to the previous taste test, leading them both to go back for more bites.

Although he admitted he couldn't put his finger on exactly what made it better, Collin said the Albertsons donut was an easy winner.

The duo didn't specify the price, but the retailer has listed 12 glazed bakery donuts for $7.99 on its website - roughly 66 cents each.

Albertsons has more than 2,200 supermarkets located across 34 states and the District of Columbia.

You can locate your nearest one by checking its store locator tool.
