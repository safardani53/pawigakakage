First pictures of five hotel workers who 'drugged and gang-raped Brit mum, 50, on family holiday in

THESE are the faces of five hotel workers accused of gang raping a British tourist while she was on holiday in Italy.

The 50-year-old widow was allegedly drugged by the men before being brutally assaulted on the last night of her trip to Meta di Sorrento.

Her 26-year-old daughter, who cannot be named for legal reasons, told the trial at Torre Annunziata near Naples that her and her mother were given spiked drinks.

The court also heard how the gang of workers were caught by cops after sharing sick videos of the attack on a WhatsApp group titled ‘Bad habits’.

The alleged victim was on holiday with her daughter to get over the recent death of her husband and was so shocked she didn’t tell cops what happened until she returned home to Kent.

Her daughter broke down in tears as she told the court: “My mum seemed distant and far away.

When I came back my mum had disappeared

“She broke down in tears at the airport as we were leaving and told me everything that happened.

“We had dinner at the hotel restaurant and shared a bottle of wine. We were with another couple of holidaymakers.

“The staff at the restaurant were very polite. Then one of the barmen offered my mother and I drink in some shot glasses.

“One of them had a tattoo on his neck. They invited us to the bar for a drink after we finished our dinner.

LIVING NIGHTMARE

“I remember having a drink and then almost immediately feeling sick. I went to the toilet and threw up.

“I was there for a while and when I came back my mum had disappeared.”

Prosecutors say the victim was taken to a room close to the swimming pool where she was raped several times by the men before stumbling back to her room at the Hotel Alimuri.

The woman reported the attack to cops in Kent when she returned and DNA samples and drug tests were sent to Italian investigators.

They revealed a high level of date rape drug GHB but lawyers for the defendants claim the victim was on tranquillisers following the death of her husband.

But her daughter told the court: “My mum has never taken any medicine like that.”

Antonino Miniero, Gennaro Davide Gargiulo, Raffaele Regio, Francesco Ciro D’Antonio, and Fabio De Virgilio all deny rape.

The trial continues.
