Aldi confirms fan favorite dinner item has been discontinued as it was a 'Find', but promises it wil

ALDI fans are disappointed after the store confirmed a special dietary dinner item has been discontinued. 

One customer complained they were “tired” of failing to find the product.

An Aldi customer tweeted the store earlier this month to complain they had not been able to locate keto dinner rolls at their local store.

“I’m tired of Aldi being out of the keto dinner rolls,” wrote the user, adding a rolling eyes emoji.

The low-carb Keto-friendly dinner rolls from the Aldi brand L’oven offered customers an alternative to bread buns.

Not only are the rolls suitable for gluten-free customers, but they were also a hit with those who prefer a low-carb diet.

Read more about Aldi

Aldi confirmed it had been discontinued the product as the rolls were an Aldi Finds item.

Aldi Finds are seasonal products that the store introduces for a limited time only but may reintroduce if they are a hit.

Aldi confirmed: “They'll be back later this winter.”

However, the customer in this case was not satisfied, saying it was “pointless” to discontinue the product at all.

MORE DISCONTINUED ITEMS

It’s not the first time recently that Aldi has disappointed customers by discontinuing a popular item.

Fans have been saddened to learn that the store’s budget alternative to Red Bull will no longer be sold at its 2,292 locations. 

Red Thunder, which was $5.49 for a pack of four cans, offered a considerably cheaper alternative to the premium energy drink, which sells for $7.24 a pack at Walmart.

One customer joked on Twitter that the store had “stole my thunder,” while another raged that the decision was “not acceptable.”

Meanwhile, Aldi has confirmed that its cheddar Jalapeño popcorn sold under the brand Clancy’s has also been discontinued.

The snack combined salty, cheesy, and spicy flavors that many customers enjoyed.

While that flavor may be gone for good, Aldi is still selling other varieties including Clancy's white cheddar cheese and movie theater butter microwave popcorn.

But Aldi isn’t the only store that has been discontinuing products.
