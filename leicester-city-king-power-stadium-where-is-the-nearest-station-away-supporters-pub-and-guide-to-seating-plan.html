Leicester City King Power stadium: Where is the nearest station, away supporters pub and guide to se

WELCOME to the home of the Premier League champions. When the King Power is rocking, there are not many better places around to enjoy a football game.

Opened in 2002 by the arguably the Foxes greatest ever striker Gary Lineker, the stadium has seen ups and down with relegation to the third tier of English Football.

Here, SunSport give you the lowdown from the fans on the essentials for a great day and night out at the football.

What is the capacity?

32,310

Club nickname?

The Foxes

Where is the nearest away pub?

Holiday Inn, directly across from the stadium (30 sec walk). Or the Counting House which is a mixed pub but away fans are welcome and it’s only about a 10 min walk away.

What is the best thing to eat? And the worst?

The pies are the best choice inside the stadium and the hot dogs are the worst.

What is the nearest train station?

Leicester railway station on London Road. It's the main station in the city and about a 15 minute car drive without traffic or a 25 minute walk. Regular buses are also available.

Any decent nightclubs to go afterwards?

Club Republic, Fan Club and Mosh.

What is the best view at the ground?

The King Power is oval shaped so the view is good wherever you sit. You pays your money and takes your choice.

And the worst?

There is no bad view at the King Power

How much for a season ticket?

For last season "new tickets started at £410 for adults, £295 for Under- 22's, £145 for under-18's and £70 for under-12's.

What is so special about the place?

Nothing special about the new ground itself but Leicester has many landmarks and most recently added to as the burial place for King Richard III.

How old is the ground?

Opened in 2002 and only a few minutes walk from the old Filbert Street ground that has now been knocked down.
