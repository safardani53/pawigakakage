Iraq War veteran Colonel Tim Collins accuses BBC of forgetting Britain won the First World War

A CELEBRATED Iraq veteran has hit out at the BBC for acting as if Britain lost the First World War.

Colonel Tim Collins, who is famous for his battle speech on the eve of the 2003 Iraq invasion, said youngsters could be forgiven for thinking Britain had lost the Great War because coverage of the conflict’s centenary had been “saturated in grief”.

He said the “brilliance” of leadership during the military campaign in 1918 had been ignored in favour of the “Blackadder version” in which cowardly and incompetent generals sent soldiers over the top to be slaughtered.

Colonel Collins said TV viewers “endure a diet of woe and horror” especially on the BBC and he has called on the broadcaster to add a note of triumph to its commemorative broadcasts on the centenary of the war this year.

He told The Radio Times: “With the centenary of the end of the First World War looming, one might not realise from the TV series recalling those momentous times that Britain and her Allies won the war.

“Last year’s coverage of the Passchendaele anniversary by the BBC was a prime example.

While paying appropriate tribute to the sacrifice of the many soldiers who lost their lives, it was saturated in grief, in the horror and pity of war.”

"We’ve aimed to reflect every aspect of the war.”
